01

Prayer is simply a conversation with your Heavenly Father. There is no formula ‚Äî speak honestly and openly. He hears every word and delights in your voice.

02

Common Questions

You might be wondering‚Ä¶

What exactly did I just do? +
You placed your trust in Jesus Christ ‚Äî acknowledging that He died for your sins, rose from the dead, and is now the Lord of your life. This is called salvation, and it is a permanent, irreversible gift. Nothing can take it away from you.
Do I have power over sin now? +
God's desire for you is freedom, not bondage. Through Christ, you have been given power over sin ‚Äî not just forgiveness of it. The Holy Spirit now lives in you, and He is greater than anything that once held you back (1 John 4:4). His grace restores you and His Spirit empowers you to walk in victory.
Do I feel different? Should I? +
Some people feel an immediate rush of peace and joy; others feel very little at first. Your salvation is not based on feelings ‚Äî it is based on God's promise, which is far more reliable. Deeper joy and transformation will naturally follow as you grow.
Should I be baptized? +
Baptism is a beautiful public declaration of your faith. It does not save you, but it is an important step of obedience and testimony. Jesus himself was baptized and commanded His followers to be as well. Talk to a pastor about taking this meaningful step.
What if my family or friends don't understand? +
Some of the people closest to you may be confused or even resistant. That's okay. Continue to love them well, live out your faith with grace, and pray for them. Your changed life will speak louder than any argument.
